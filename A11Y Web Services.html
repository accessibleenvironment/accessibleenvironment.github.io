Welcome:

Welcome to our platform, where we empower you to build websites that are not just visually stunning but also accessible to everyone, in compliance with WCAG standards.

Keeping Accessibility in Mind.

Embarking on the creation of content for a website that empowers users to build their own accessible websites is truly a commendable initiative. Explore our sample content below, which underscores the utilization of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and ARIA attributes to guarantee compliance with WCAG standards, specifically focusing on achieving the esteemed Level AA (2.1/2.2).

About Us:

we believe in the importance of inclusivity on the web. Our platform is designed to guide you through the process of creating websites that meet WCAG 2.1/2.2 Level AA accessibility standards. We prioritize the use of native HTML tags and elements, CSS for design, and JavaScript for interactive features.

Why Accessibility Matters:

Accessibility is not just a checkbox; it's about ensuring that your website is usable by everyone, regardless of their abilities. By following WCAG standards, you contribute to a web that is welcoming and inclusive.

ARIA Attributes:
When native HTML tags or elements are insufficient, integrate ARIA attributes to enhance accessibility.
Use ARIA roles (role) and properties (aria-*) judiciously to convey information to assistive technologies.
